Jaipur ~ Pink City

Jaipur founded in 1726, is the capital and largest city in Rajastan. The city was painted pink to welcome the Prince of Wales, later Edward VII, in 1876. Many of the avenues remain painted in pink, giving Jaipur a distinctive pink appearance. ~~ A MUST SEE: City Palace, a 18th-century palace of Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, now a museum & royal residence. ~~

Jodhpur ~ Blue City

Jodpur founded in 1459, is the oldest but second largest city in Rajastan. The city has many buildings painted blue because it is believed to help reflect the intense summer heat. Another popular reason is that the priestly class in India wanted to distinguish their homes from everyone else's. ~~ A MUST SEE: Mehrangarh Fort, one of the largest forts in India. Built around 1460 by Rao Jodha, the fort is situated 410 feet above the city and is enclosed by imposing thick walls. The fort now functions as a museum. ~~

Udaipur ~ White City

Udaipur, is home to 007- James Bond movie! Founded in 1553. Is considered to be the Venice of the East, as it has the most lakes in this region. The white marble buildings has given this city its nickname. ~~ A MUST SEE: Udaipur Solar Observatory, on an island in the Fateh Sagar Lake. The sky conditions at Udaipur are quite favorable for solar observations. ~~
